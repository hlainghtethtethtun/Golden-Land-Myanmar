Patala instruments are the Wa Patala (Wood Xylophone). The similar instruments could be found under different names Cambodia (roneat) and in Thailand (ranaad).

Saung Gauk

An arched harp, called a saung-gauk, with a long neck carved from a tree root, from which 13 silk strings would have extended from tuning pegs to the body.

Tayaw

Gilded and painted Burmese stringed instrument (tayaw); with a carved gilded bird sitting on the top of the neck. It rests on stand carved to look like a snake. The bow is also gilded and painted and strung with hair.

Flute

The palwei flute is a wind instrument which consists of a hollow tube played by blowing through a hole at one end. There are two kinds of Myanmar flutes, the khin palwei and the kyaw palwei.

Pat Waing

The pat waing or pat wang is a drum circle instrument used in the Burmese folk musical ensemble.The player sits in the middle of a horseshoe-shaped shell made of elaborately carved wood and decorated with gold leaf.

Shan Osi

Shan Osi (Shan long drum) is traditional symbol remark for Shan ethnic.The Kainari and Kainara are mythological creatures, part-human and part-bird. These duet birds dance is the famous Shan tradition dance.
